Back Exercises Based on Your Daily Pain Rating

This program is meant for the good and bad days. You will feel the best results if you can log in 3 or more times per week. If it's a bad pain day - no problem. The exercises will be more gentle and restorative. If you are feeling good - great! Let's push it!

Rate your pain today!

1 = Very minimal pain...10 = extremely painful.

Sample Exercises

Exercise One

Seated Glute Stretch

Sit upright in a sturdy chair. Place your right ankle on your left thigh, just above your knee. Place your hands on your shins. Keeping your spine straight, lean slightly forward to deepen the stretch. Hold for 45 seconds. Return to the starting position. Repeat with the other leg.

Exercise Two

Double Leg Bridge

Lie on your back, with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground. Tighten your abdomial muscles. Lift your hips off the ground until your knees, hips and shoulders form a straight line. Hold your bridged position for a couple of seconds before easing back down. Repeat 10-12 times.

Exercise Three

Elevated Plank

Place your forearms or palms on a flat, elevated surface with your elbows directly under your shoulders. Walk your feet back until your body forms a straight line on a diagonal. Pull your abs in as you tuck your tailbone slightly and actively press into your forearms or palms. Hold for 30-45 seconds.
